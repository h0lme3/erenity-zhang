Founder Elisabeth’s Self Statement

The first 16 years of my life spent in a rapid-growing, economy-centered, generally monolingual eastern country, China, being vegan in my non-vegan friendly hometown Hangzhou dated back in 2014, and under the spiritual guidance of veganism, consequently exploring Kantian philosophy and the intersection between Buddhism and Christianity during a volunteering trip to Nepal as an English teacher, were proofs that I am outsider of traditional Chinese education system. Although, like a typical good Chinese student, I partook in leadership roles, math Olympiads, and public speech and performing arts contests, all my traumas and dramas inevitably tied to my home, hometown and home country propelled me to learn politics, Sino-US relations, and feminist studies. In the process, mathematics has been my light of intellectual guidance and pillar of life. During my study in UC San Deigo in 2018 Fall, I fell into the victim of crimes- cyberstalking and sexual harassment derived from the technological method. Helpless, depressing, and for me “all is over”. 2020, I initiated anti-sexual harassment project and was an advocate for human rights in China. Things have been working well! In the same year June anti-sexual harassment law was published first time in the history; 2021 Jan, human rights journal brought up the digital privacy rights issue and in August, Personal Information Protection Law was born.

2021 July, the idea of E-renity was born when I was in Shanghai and I decide to seriously found a project. I want to mark the half milestone of me fighting against hackers.

Through cyberstalking I learned how evildoers disguise themselves and the precautions we need to take in this digital age. The curiosity to learn cryptography in end-to-end encryption, had me finished a textbook when I was on a flight to London to escape stalking in US. When I talked to people about the experience that shoot me down from the intellectual, democratic cushion for elites, and the cause to found the project, I often find those who’re interested in the illuminating sides of the project, can’t empathise with me; those who empathise with me, can’t help me. It seems the volume of sadness in my story is simply too huge that they couldn'’'t possibly partake. I agree, I can’t take so much either. I’m a human being. I need help, but I'm alone, so-said talented yet so alone. But does the world not need me? At least my home country says “NO”.

All the sadness has an end in China. So did mine. This is a country with heavy history and was once tarnished, bullied, robbed and now, despite being defamed and its people being discriminated abroad, it’s been rebuilt, re-prospered, and stepped into a truly innovative age. I would hence declare my rights based on my own suffering that all is equal and technological control has a limit.

Talk to me for project details. I am right here, alive, and I will eventually have my book, my movie, and I want everyone to achieve their best in their life. I see one’s value and I bet on it - “I exist because I want to”

Together for a better tomorrow.

Dating & Cyberstalking

The story starts from the famous dating app, Okcupid, which was founded by a group of Havard Math graduates to facilitate dating for like-minded singles whose majors are in STEM as well. I, as an aspiring mathematician at that time, July 2017, downloaded this famous app and started dating in California. At the time it was portrayed by the medias as a successful dating story with the mathematician from UCLA Chris McKinlay hack to Okcupid to get 30,000 matches.

A year later, December 2018, I met my cyberstalker who compromised Okcupid platform, gaining my personal details after already being benefitted from a massive hack in Apple’s iCloud database (in which case Apple was undergoing its very first historical invent in cyber emergency), and my cyberstalker, who had more than ample information, started to stalk, intimidate and terrorise me using Okcupid platform by writing my real life events on random dating profiles of strangers, combining my personal details to make it only identifiable to me. In the next year February, we finally heard news from TechCrunch that Okcupid’s server got hacked resulting in millions of users information being leaked.

I fought this cyberstalking in the entire 2019 alone and have been the advocate for cyberstalking and written reports to not only the police, but Apple, US Federal Trade Union and other tech companies about their security flaws. I received no follow-ups and no solid help.

Now, the dating method employed in successful stories since 2017 has suggested a dark side. Hacking a dating server, once the glory, now a shame.

In the beginning of 2020, I saw Apple silently put a “report a bug” feature on their official website which encourages reports from consumers of any security bugs in IOS/OS systems. I wonder if my passionate letter in 2019, written in a lonely time in Scotland, travelling, escaping from distress, actually worked for Apple to realise that, things have not been perfect.

Two years later, in 2021, I met my so-called “Mr.Lucky”, who was, also, hacking a dating app- Tinder. Over a video call, this charming man showed me how he created a software to fetch all female faces on Tinder and pick the one he likes best. This suggests a data breach from Tinder’s backend. We met through Tinder, talked on Telegram, and ended our correspondence with my experience of cyberstalking and hacking in the last 3 years- the truth turned out to be, if the golden time has a place to stop, as if what was praised at first would last, he was yet another data scientist hacking a dating platform. Anyone who has the knowledge about programming and hacking will be able to see through what’s inside everyone’s inbox on Tinder or Okcupid (of course the same as emails).

I and Mr. Lucky ended up arguing, about mathematics every time. If not math, then it was always some other intellectual point. However, in his lens, I was the math girl he found amongst probably another 30,000 matches of women. When my math fellows from Harvard created Okcupid, have they thought about how many more bright, young and ambitious mathematicians, if not physicists, would be fished, hacked, slightly stalked or otherwise severely cyberstalked, whilst enjoying all of this intellectual encounters and interesting romances?

The same as Tor, GitHub, and many other forums and platforms, they stand neutral and let people use what they can.

E-renity is not a neutral platform. We firmly protect our academia’s spirit and drive to explore the world morally, we stand by our pure theorists who didn’t quite have the time to learn the technical skills required for cybersecurity. I personally was one of whom living only in the highly abstract mathematical world that once hitting on the cold ground of technological abuse, I was losing all of the orderly components of my being. I seized my bright side with tremendous efforts and now want to create this platform.

E-renity, however, is open to all. We stand always in the light, but we do appreciate if red hackers turn white and would like to work in cybersecurity and protect citizens instead. We understand that it would be a hard journey to emerge from the shadow and look at this world differently. We have something for you to consider, and a job offer is guaranteed after completing our special designed test and individually tailored program. Again, this is for everyone.

E-renity

E-renity is Yintelligentsia`s first project. We create a platform between social and tech sectors to improve the world`s singlemost stressing problem- cybersecurity. Through individually tailored programs all professions from social sectors are able to learn from tech sectors from a free awareness improvement to hands-on technical skills.

E-renity is a project invented by CEO Elisabeth and based on her real victim experience in cyberstalking when she was at her 3rd year university of UC San Diego. This project is going to completely change the societies and rebuild western definition of "trust" immediately after it is out on the stage. However after China published Personal Information Protection Law in August, 2021 and with more demand on internet safety and privacy concern rise, the market here is also growing.

The creation of such a platform to connect people from tech industry and social sector together to work on cybersecurity with the latest, most advanced datas and news- it's a strong, effective union and eventually will give birth to our unique security products based on our databases through years of experience after startup, then Yintelligentsia will be the single most authoritative source for cyber defence after covid.

In short, the part of E-renity that concerns psychology and socio-economic changes demonstrating on victim support programs overlaps with one crucial part of EndAbuse.
