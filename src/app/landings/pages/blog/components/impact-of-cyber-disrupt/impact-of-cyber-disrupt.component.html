Impacts of Cyber Disruptions on Our Society

Cyber attacks are becoming common day by day as our Critical infrastructures, economies, and societies are largely dependent on computer networking. These are becoming more popular as our need for technology increases with time, especially after the Covid-19 Pandemic.

A Symantec Cybercrime report published back in 2012 suggests that the cost of the revenues we lose each year due to cyber-attacks has reached up to $114 Billion. In contrast, if the amount of time and money spent to recover from these cyber attacks is counted, it shows some staggering figures. It costs more than $385 Billion per year.

Why are Cyber-Attacks so Flourishing?

There is a very simple answer to this question; it is not easy to find out who attacked you. Cyber-attacks are easier and less risky than physical attacks. Although, these might hurt you more than the physical attacks. According to the research, cyber-attacks are not going to slow down easily.

Who is more affected by the Cyber Attacks?

These cyber-attacks affect everyone living in our society. There are businesses, banks, offices etc., all of them have been victims of these attacks. The data is leaked through fake emails, malware and social media. Businesses have lost billions of dollars each year by losing their confidential and financial data.

These attacks are not only restricted to these areas. The hackers always do hacks for a purpose. Doesn't matter if they want to leak personal information or do some kind of stealing. They do it, and the Internet provides them with a lot weaker links to go forward.

Effect of Cyber-attacks on Students

On the other hand, cyber-attacks are not only confined to financial losses. They can hurt even more, and it happens when toxic information is spread through different means. This type of attack mostly targets students who are using tons of their time on the Internet. It can lead them to read or find out wrong information that can cause more problems for the society. There is vicious news about the cyberattacks that happened in the pandemic. The victims of these cyber attacks are found to be the students mostly.

Cybercriminals or hackers targeted students this past year for their attacks. A report says more than 1600 schools, colleges and other educational institutes targeted ransomware only in 2020. These attacks cost more than $2.73 Million to the educational institutes.

In July 2020, the sensitive data of more than 400000 students from the US educational institutes were stolen by hackers and put on sale on different hacker forums. This data includes personal information, contact details, email addresses, and home address.

There is a need to compose plans to restrict hackers from doing whatever they are doing to harm society. It can only be done by planning strict rules and regulations and making the Internet a safe place for the people. There should not be any weak links in networking. The whole world is connected to one place, i.e., the Internet. So, what's required is compatibility. If a hacker is doing his job, there must be someone more capable than the hacker himself to stop these crimes.
