Digital Economy in Post Covid During Cyber Disruptions

Before covid, the world would shift to digitization, but the pandemic accelerated the shift. If there were any doubts regarding the need or necessity of all the businesses to shift to online business, the pandemic has closed them. During covid, all the business was closed due to lockdown, curfews and shutdowns, which increased the need for digital business. So, the businesses that could not run physically due to the Covid-19 pandemic were shifted online. These digital technologies played an important role in low and middle-income countries.

In addition to that, new online businesses were also introduced to facilitate the people sitting at home to buy things online. With the increase in the digital economy, cyber disruptions also increased. As people started using online platforms, they usually asked for users’ personal information for registration. So, cyber disruptions started increasing rapidly. User’s personal information can be hacked easily, so there is a huge chance of loss of information or data from online businesses like online stores, banks, hospitals, and education sectors. However, our utmost duty is to provide security to our user’s data and information. This issue could only be solved if serious steps were taken to increase digital serenity.

As digitization is increasing and the technology is getting more advanced, new ways are introduced to hack any website or application very easily. To protect ourselves and our businesses from these security threats, making new digital strategies and implementing them is necessary. As digital technology is spreading, the risk of the impact of cyber-attacks increases. For this purpose, Erenity is a platform that connects tech companies with different social sectors or industries with high-security needs. It closes all the lack of linkages between social and technical sectors.

Lack of Linkage Between Social and Technical Sectors:

The pandemic proved to be a big challenge for businesses operating worldwide. Most of the business in the world operates through information technology. Different types of digital devices, cloud systems, and data centers play a vital role in running the online business. This kind of technology is lucrative and a bigger target for different kinds of cybercriminals.

The world is now more digitally connected than it was many years ago. Video conferences and personal meetings were also held online back then. Previously, the organizations required employees to be together physically, but now strong internet connection is required to operate businesses as all businesses have now shifted to online. Now mostly worldwide operating businesses have their meetings online. So, businesses depend on different digital services in order to retain the supply chain for fundamentals while reducing social contact. They are utilizing different digital channels to pay salaries to employees.

Hence, cyber security should be upgraded to prevent any crisis from occurring. During covid, several cybercriminals exploit cyber security as the remote working has been increased via online meetings, both video and voice calls. During the Covid-19 pandemic, personal meetings were held online via call or text. So, the risk of cyber-attacks increased rapidly.

Between February 2020 and May 2020, half a million people experienced cyberattacks in which personal data and information were stolen and sold on the dark web. The digital economy produces new opportunities in low or middle-income countries. As these countries aren’t much advanced, their security is also low, and they can’t provide high security to the users. Before the pandemic, retailers used to provide goods physically. Still, due to covid, businesses started using social media apps like WhatsApp and Facebook to provide goods to the customers at their homes. Some businesses build their applications, so they need strong, secure and advanced applications.

Rapid and unplanned digitization increased the risk and impact of cyberattacks. Before covid, there was a lack of linkage between social and technical sectors. Still, due to covid, the remote working environment increased rapidly, so the linkage got weaker and weaker. Talking about the security breach and loss of data of the users, the hackers use credential stuffing, a form of cyberattack where hackers use the previously stolen usernames and passwords and get access to other accounts of that person. This is possible because usually, people use usernames and passwords for different accounts.

It is also noticed that unknown members joined virtual meetings of some businesses and got to know about the personal or sensitive information of the company. The information is then sold or misused for some negative cause or made available to the public to damage the company’s reputation. So, these online businesses are under immense pressure to protect their companies from these cyber-attacks. Companies hire white hat hackers who try to break the security wall of the company’s application and go into the website or application just to know the weaknesses that should be removed to improve the security.
